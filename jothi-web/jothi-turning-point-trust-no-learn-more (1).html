About Us

A Place of Hope and Healing

JOTHI Turning Point Trust Rehabilitation Center is committed to helping individuals overcome alcohol and drug addiction through a structured, compassionate, and holistic recovery program built on dignity and long-term care.

Our Mission

To bring positive, lasting change to lives affected by addiction through structured treatment, counseling, and community support.

Our Vision

A society where every individual struggling with addiction finds the path back to a healthy, meaningful, addiction-free life.

Founder

Mr. Mohanraj

Founder & Managing Trustee

A dedicated social worker with over 13 years of experience in addiction counseling and community service, Mr. Mohanraj founded JOTHI Turning Point Trust in 2016 to give individuals struggling with addiction a genuine path to recovery. His work extended into large-scale COVID-19 relief efforts, providing food, essentials, and support to vulnerable communities, and continues to guide the Trust's mission of compassionate, structured rehabilitation.

Comprehensive Care for Complete Recovery

At JOTHI Turning Point Trust, we provide a wide range of services designed to heal the mind, body, and spirit — every program is built around the individual, not just the addiction.

Focused recovery support addressing both the physical habit and the underlying behavioral patterns of cannabis dependence.

FAQs

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before reaching out.

How long is the treatment program?

Our minimum program duration is 3 months, with the exact length depending on individual assessment and progress during recovery.

Is admission available 24×7?

Yes, admission support is available around the clock. You can call or WhatsApp us any time for immediate assistance.

What facilities are available at the center?

Residents have access to comfortable accommodation, nutritious meals, 24×7 nursing care, weekly doctor visits, yoga, meditation, indoor games, and a recovery library.

Do you provide follow-up support after discharge?

Yes, every individual receives one full year of structured follow-up support to help prevent relapse and support independent living.
